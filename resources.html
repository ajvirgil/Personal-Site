Favorite Resources

Here is a nifty list of some of my favorite websites/resources I use (almost) everyday that help me get my work done.

Tools

SKETCH
Sketch is my go to tool when it comes to design. I even created a "Wireframe" toolkit to load into my project to eliminate the need to use traditional wireframming tools like Balsamiq.

BRACKETS
Yep. The code editor that Adobe made. Who woulda thought? The "Live Preview" is a killer feature and helps speed up my development process imensley.

MARVEL
I don't always use prototyping tools, but when I'm in a rush and need to show how something will "work", I use Marvel. (Note: InVision is also great, but Marvel edged it out here with it's $0 price tag)

MOLESKINE & WHITEBOARD
Nearly 100% of my projects start out as an analog sketches. I usually start by writing down ideas in my Moleskine. Once its kind of fleshed out I move to a big-ass whiteboard and piece things together.

Inspiration

HTTPSTER
There are tons of sites that showcase the best (web) design the world has to offer. However, httpster made this list solely because their name made me chuckle. (Also check out: SiteInspire, Awwwards, & The Best Designs)

BUTDOESITFLOAT
Most of my inspiration doesn't come from graphic/web design, but from traditional art. BDIF has curated a beautiful collection of highbrow art for your eyes to feast upon. (Other great ones: Designers Go To Heaven & The Fox Is Black)

CREATIVE MORNINGS
TED Talks, but for highly specific subjects in the realm of design, creativity, typography, & more. My favorite talk here.

DESIGNERS.MX
I'm constantly listening to music when I'm working. It helps calibrate me and put me in a creative flow. Designers.mx is a tool to discover music that other designers are listening to to get inspired.

Organization

DROPBOX
I hardly save anything to my physical hard drive anymore. Everything is in the #cloud. I love to use Dropbox for this.

PINTEREST
I've been on Pinterest since it's closed beta days. It's fantastic for creating mood boards and gathering & visualizing data.

GOOGLE DOCS
I used to use Evernote to do this, but they got a nasty case of feature overload, so I jumped ship to Google. Excellent way to simply organize notes, thoughts, and (ugh)spreadsheets.

BRANDFOLDER
This is a great tool to organize a finalized brand identity along with all it's supporting assets like typefaces, colors, & more.

Communication

MAILBOX (RIP)
Email is horrible, but Mailbox is working hard to change that. The swiping and snoozing features makes dealing with email actually bareable. Whoa!

SLACK
This is a newer tool in my aresenal. It's helped eliminate the headache of communicating via email and the unorganizable mess of using text messages.

TRELLO
Trello has recently dethroned my long time favorite to-do app, Clear, with it's ability to show me all my tasks and help me move them along into different lists. R.I.P. CLEAR.

SUNRISE (RIP)
Not really a "communication" tool, per se, but Trello's integration with the beautifully designed Sunrise makes my life a whole lot easier.

Freelance

BEAGLE
Beagle is a great tool for creating beautiful proposals to send to clients. It's still in beta, so it will only get better and more expensive from here!

BONSAI
A great tool for creating contracts for designers. It helps get that neccessary but boring businessy stuff outta the way so you can get back to doing what you love: zooming in at 900% to align a rectangle to another rectangle.

TOGGL
Toggl is the hands down easiest way to track your projects and billable hours. Yep, that's it.

SLIMVOICE
Getting paid to design is awesome. Dealing with fugly invoice systems isn't. Enter Slimvoice, the dead simple invoice tool that makes getting your $$$ fun.

Misc.

DESIGNER NEWS
Great hub for finding new products/articles/whatever. WARNING: never read the comments.

BOURBON
Bourbon (and the whole Bourbon family) has been my go to for website frameworks. It's Sassy and easy to learn.

COOLORS
A pretty cool (get it) site that helps you find some fantastic color palettes.

DRIBBBLE
Great for looking at 800 differnt versions of "Work Hard & Stay Humble"
